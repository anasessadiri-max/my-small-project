Furniture Care and Maintenance

How to protect, clean, and extend the life of your furniture

Why Furniture Care Matters

Furniture is one of the most valuable parts of any home. Without proper care, furniture can wear out quickly due to dust, moisture, sunlight exposure, and daily use. Understanding how to maintain furniture helps preserve its appearance, comfort, and structural strength for many years.

Common Furniture Problems in Homes

  • Scratches and surface damage
  • Fading caused by sunlight
  • Cracks in wood or leather
  • Fabric stains and odors
  • Loose joints or weakened structure

Basic Furniture Care Tips

Simple habits such as regular dusting, using protective pads, avoiding direct sunlight, and cleaning spills immediately can significantly reduce furniture damage over time.

When Furniture Needs Professional Care

Deep cracks, severe discoloration, structural weakness, or mold growth on furniture may require professional repair or restoration services.

Prevention and Long-Term Maintenance

  • Rotate furniture placement to avoid uneven wear
  • Use furniture covers when needed
  • Control indoor humidity levels
  • Clean using materials suitable for each surface
  • Inspect furniture regularly for early signs of damage
